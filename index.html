Section 1

About Popislicious

Our Business

Popislicious is a food-based business practicum focused on selling freshly made popia goreng (fried spring rolls) at UMS campus. We offer a crunchy, affordable, and delicious snack loved by students and staff alike.


Food & Beverage Snack / Street Food

📋

Our Products & Services

We sell two main product sizes:


  • 🌯 5 pieces — RM5 (Original, No Toppings)
  • 🌯 10 pieces — RM9
  • 🧁 Toppings +RM1 — Milky Choco / Matcha Glaze / Sweet Cheese
  • 🎉 Mix Toppings +RM1.50

We also offer delivery (COD) and pop-up booth events on campus.

🏫

Industry & Context

This business was conducted as part of the Ekspo APK practicum under UMS, Group 9, Section 5 APK. The practicum simulates real-world entrepreneurship through hands-on product selling, marketing, and financial management.

Section 3

How We Promoted

📸

Instagram

Posted teasers, hard-sell and soft-sell content on @popislicious. Grew to 300+ followers through consistent posting and engaging visuals.

💬

WhatsApp

Used WhatsApp status updates and group chats to reach classmates and friends. Accepted preorders via WhatsApp DM.

👥

Facebook

Shared posts on Facebook to reach a wider campus audience including lecturers and older students.

🗣️

Word of Mouth

Happy customers referred us to their friends, which became one of our most effective organic growth channels.

🛒

Pop-Up Booth

Set up a physical booth at Gazebo FPPS from 12pm–3pm on event day, attracting walk-in customers and live sales.

🎨

Digital Posters

Created eye-catching digital promotional posters with our brand aesthetic to announce new flavours, preorders, and booth dates.

📣 Content Strategy: We used a mix of teaser posts (build anticipation), hard-sell posts (direct product promotion with price), and soft-sell posts (lifestyle, behind-the-scenes, customer features) to keep our audience engaged without feeling overwhelmed by promotions.

Section 6

Challenges & Solutions

❗ Problems We Faced

  • Managing preorder demand — high order volume made it difficult to prepare enough stock on time.
  • Coordinating delivery schedules with busy class schedules of all group members.
  • Limited budget for initial ingredient purchases, affecting batch size.
  • Getting consistent social media engagement in the early stages of the account.
  • Weather and venue conditions affecting our pop-up booth setup.

✅ Our Solutions

  • Implemented a preorder cut-off system via WhatsApp to control demand and prepare stock in advance.
  • Created a delivery roster assigning specific members to delivery duties each day.
  • Pooled group member funds initially and tracked expenses carefully to manage budget.
  • Used a mix of content strategies — reels, stories, and interactive polls — to boost engagement.
  • Prepared a backup indoor location and brought a portable pop-up tent for weather contingencies.
Section 7

Suggestions & Recommendations

What we'd do better next time 🌱

01

Expand to e-commerce platforms such as Shopee or GrabFood to reach a wider customer base beyond campus, enabling more consistent revenue and easier order management.

02

Introduce loyalty rewards for repeat customers (e.g., "Buy 5, get 1 free" punch cards) to improve customer retention and encourage word-of-mouth marketing.

03

Improve financial tracking by using a simple digital spreadsheet or app (like Wave) from day one, making it easier to generate accurate income statements and break-even analysis.

04

Create video content (Reels/TikTok) showing the preparation process and taste reactions. Video content consistently outperforms static posts in reach and engagement.

05

Plan booth events earlier and secure bookings in advance to get better locations and avoid last-minute setup issues. Consider partnering with other student groups for joint events.
